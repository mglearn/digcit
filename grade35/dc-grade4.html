Teacher launch · Grade 4 · TEKS §126.9

The Permanent Record

A student thought old posts would just disappear. They didn't. Crack four locks to learn why the internet remembers.

Launch student activity Correlation

The premise

Months ago, a student posted photos, sent messages, and used a picture that was not theirs. Today they found all of it still online. Read the clues, then open all four locks. Each lock has a reason that shows why the answer is right.

Standards alignment

Aligned to Texas TEKS Technology Applications, Grade 4, §126.9, Digital Citizenship strand — subsections (c)(10), (c)(8), (c)(9). Skills are paraphrased; read the official standard at tea.texas.gov. This page shows the reasoning focus of each lock — not the answers.

In the classroom

  • Warm up (5 min). Ask: "Be a Records Detective" — what does this mean online? Surface prior experience before the activity.
  • Model one clue. Open a single clue together and think aloud: what does this tell us, and which lock might it help?
  • Reason, don't guess. Require students to say why before checking a lock. After each solve, read the revealed reason and compare it to their thinking.
  • Spot the decoy. One clue is true but useless. Debrief which one and how they knew — a core evaluation skill.
  • Language support. The 🌐 picker offers 7 languages; pair newcomers to discuss clues in their home language, then answer.
  • Extend. Have students write a new clue, or a real-life example, for the focus skill they found hardest.
