Teacher launch · Grade 6 · TEKS §126.17

Fair Use Detective

A student creator wants to remix a photo, a song clip, and a quote into a class project — but is it legal? Follow the evidence and crack four locks to close the case.

Open activity (licensed) Correlation

The premise

A creator named Rivka is building a video and does not know which pieces she is actually allowed to reuse. Read the clues, weigh the evidence, and open all four locks. Each lock ends with a reason that proves why the answer is correct.

Standards alignment

Aligned to Texas TEKS Technology Applications, Grade 6, §126.17, Digital Citizenship strand — subsections (c)(10), (c)(9). Skills are paraphrased; read the official standard at tea.texas.gov. This page shows the reasoning focus of each lock — not the answers.

In the classroom

  • Warm up (5 min). Ask: "Open the Case File" — what does this mean online? Surface prior experience before the activity.
  • Model one clue. Open a single clue together and think aloud: what does this tell us, and which lock might it help?
  • Reason, don't guess. Require students to say why before checking a lock. After each solve, read the revealed reason and compare it to their thinking.
  • Spot the decoy. One clue is true but useless. Debrief which one and how they knew — a core evaluation skill.
  • Language support. The 🌐 picker offers 7 languages; pair newcomers to discuss clues in their home language, then answer.
  • Extend. Have students write a new clue, or a real-life example, for the focus skill they found hardest.
